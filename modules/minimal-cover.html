Equivalent FD Sets & Minimal Cover

Two FD sets are equivalent when each implies the other ‚ÄĒ they constrain the data identically. Among all equivalent sets, the minimal (canonical) cover is the leanest: every right side is a single attribute, no left side carries an extraneous attribute, and no FD is redundant. It's the clean starting point for normalization.

5Pitfalls & exam tips

‚ö†ÔłŹ Common mistakes

  • Doing the steps out of order ‚ÄĒ always: (1) single RHS, (2) trim LHS, (3) remove redundant FDs.
  • When testing redundancy of X‚ÜíY, use the closure under all the other FDs, not the full set.
  • Minimal cover is not unique ‚ÄĒ different orders can yield different (but equivalent) covers.
  • Trimming LHS and removing FDs at the same time ‚ÄĒ do all LHS trimming first, then redundancy.

ūüí° Exam tips

  • To prove F ‚Č° G: show every FD of G follows from F, and every FD of F follows from G ‚ÄĒ using closures.
  • Single-attribute RHS first makes the later steps much easier to reason about.
  • An attribute is extraneous in XA‚ÜíY if X‚ÜíY already holds under the set.
  • The minimal cover preserves all dependencies ‚ÄĒ it's the canonical input to 3NF synthesis.
